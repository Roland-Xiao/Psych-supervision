# Supervision Session Structure Template

> Designed for 60-minute individual supervision sessions.
> Scale proportionally for 30- or 90-minute sessions.

*Source: Integrates Kadushin's three-function supervision model and the Hawkins & Shohet framework.*

---

## Opening (0–5 min)

**Supervisor asks:**

- "How are you doing this week? How's your working state?"
- "What do you most want to focus on today?"

**Purpose:** Establish the therapist's current state before moving into content. The state the therapist brings into supervision is itself clinical data.

---

## Case Presentation and Exploration (5–30 min)

**Presentation guidelines:**

- Present a **specific moment** (a concrete interaction), not an overall summary
- If a verbatim transcript or session recording exists (with client consent), that is preferable
- Describe your internal experience, not only what the client did

**Core supervisory exploration questions** — select 2–3:

```
Internal process:
  □  "What was happening in your body when that moment occurred?"
  □  "If your reaction were a message, what would it be saying?"
  □  "Have you noticed something you keep not mentioning?"
  □  "In that moment, what do you think the client was seeing in you?"

Relational:
  □  "Where does the alliance stand right now?"
  □  "Who does this client remind you of?"
  □  "What is the one thing you least want to say to this client?"
  □  "If this client suddenly stopped coming tomorrow, what would be your first reaction?"

Conceptualization:
  □  "In one sentence, how would you describe this person's core struggle?"
  □  "What is this symptom protecting?"
  □  "If change occurred, what would this client lose?"
  □  "Five years from now, if treatment succeeds, what does this person's life look like?"
```

---

## Deepening the Work (30–50 min)

Based on the case, choose **one** of the following directions to pursue:

### Direction A — Parallel process exploration

If a distinctive atmosphere or repeating pattern emerges in the supervision room:

1. **Name it:** *"I notice something about the feel of our conversation right now... I'm not entirely sure why."*
2. **Explore:** *"Could this be mirroring what's happening in the therapy room?"*
3. **Transform:** *"How we navigate this right now may point toward a direction in the treatment."*

### Direction B — Using countertransference

| Step | Question |
|------|---------|
| Sense | What is happening inside me / in my body right now? *(without judgment)* |
| Distinguish | Does this come more from me, or from the client? |
| Understand | If it comes from the client, what does it reveal? |
| Use | How can this information inform an intervention? |

### Direction C — Case conceptualization reconstruction

Re-examine the case from a new theoretical perspective. Refer to `assets/case_conceptualization.md`.

### Direction D — Discussing specific interventions

- What are you planning to do in the next session? Why now? Why this particular intervention?
- Is the alliance solid enough to support the depth of this intervention?

---

## Integration and Application (50–55 min)

**Supervisor asks:**

- "What are you taking away from today's discussion?"
- "What do you want to try in the next session?"
- "Is there anything that still feels unresolved?"

---

## Supervisory Relationship and Closing (55–60 min)

**Supervisor asks:**

- "How did today's supervision feel for you?"
- "Is there anything you want to say about our supervisory work itself?"

**Close by leaving one question** — not a summary, but an opening:

> Send the therapist away with a question to carry. Good supervision does not give you answers; it sends you away with better questions.

---

## After the Session: Therapist Self-Check

```
□  Today's supervision left me feeling...
□  Is my current caseload sustainable?
□  Are there signs of vicarious trauma that need attention?
□  The focus for next supervision will be...
```
