# Major Therapy Frameworks: Reference Guide

> **Load condition:** When comparing therapies or recommending treatment directions.
>
> Every therapy has its range of application. There is no "best therapy" — only the therapy most suited to this person at this moment.

## 1. Quick-Reference Therapy Overview

| Therapy | Change mechanism | Role of therapeutic relationship | Strongest applications | Typical duration | Key limitations |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------------------|------------------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Classical Psychoanalysis** | Making the unconscious conscious; working through | Neutral container; transference mirror | Personality disorders; chronic issues | Years | Time-intensive; costly; requires high client functioning |
| **Brief Psychodynamic (BPT)** | Focus on Core Conflictual Relationship Theme | Active; limited self-disclosure | Depression; relational difficulties | 16–40 sessions | Requires adequate psychological functioning |
| **Object Relations** | Restructuring internalized object relations | Relationship itself is the primary medium | BPD; NPD | Years | — |
| **CBT** | Correcting cognitive distortions and avoidant behavior | Collaborative coach | Depression; anxiety; OCD | 8–20 sessions | Limited for deep personality change |
| **DBT** | Skill acquisition + dialectical acceptance | Dialectical balance; team support | BPD; self-harm; substance use | 6–12 months | Resource-intensive |
| **ACT** | Psychological flexibility; values-driven action | Peer-like; therapist models alongside | Chronic pain; anxiety; depression | 8–16 sessions | — |
| **EMDR** | Adaptive Information Processing; trauma reprocessing | Safe container | PTSD; acute trauma | 8–12 sessions | Stage 1 stabilization required first |
| **Person-Centered (PCT)** | Unconditional positive regard activates self-actualization | The relationship itself is the therapy | Growth; mild-to-moderate depression | Open-ended | Severe symptoms may need additional approaches |
| **Gestalt** | Here-and-now awareness; completing unfinished gestalts | Genuine contact; therapist participates | Emotional distress; relational work | Open-ended | — |
| **EFT (Emotion-Focused)** | Deepening and transforming emotion | Empathic resonance | Depression; couples | 16–20 sessions | — |
| **Narrative Therapy** | Reauthoring dominant narratives; externalizing the problem | Curious collaborator | Identity issues; oppression-related concerns | 10–20 sessions | — |
| **SFBT (Solution-Focused)** | Activating client resources | Empowering | Short-term specific problems | 6–10 sessions | Limited for deep underlying issues |
| **Somatic Experiencing (SE)** | Releasing trauma-bound body energy | Safe accompaniment | PTSD; somatization | Open-ended | Requires specialized training |
| **Satir Model** | Transforming self-worth and communication patterns | Warm authority | Family; relationships; self-worth | Open-ended | — |
| **MBSR / MBCT** | Metacognitive awareness; decentering | Teacher / fellow practitioner | Depression relapse prevention; chronic stress | 8-week structured | Requires client discipline in self-practice |
| **CFT (Compassion-Focused)** | Activating the soothing system; countering self-criticism | Warm; non-judgmental | Shame; self-criticism; trauma | 12–20 sessions | — |
| **IFS (Internal Family Systems)** | Integrating internal parts; returning to Self-leadership | Peer + witness | Complex trauma; identity issues | Open-ended | Requires client acceptance of a pluralistic mind model |
| **MBT (Mentalization-Based)** | Developing mentalizing capacity | Inquiring relational stance | BPD; attachment difficulties | 12–18 months | — |
| **Sandplay / Play Therapy** | Three-dimensional presentation and integration of the unconscious | Witness; safe space | Children; trauma; pre-verbal clients | Open-ended | Requires specialized training and equipment |

## 3. Core Change Mechanisms Compared

All effective therapies share these common elements, expressed differently across modalities:

| Change mechanism | How it appears across therapies |
|-----------------|----------------------------------|
| **Emotional activation** | Present in all therapies (at different depths); trauma processing requires staying within window of tolerance |
| **Emotional regulation** | DBT skills; mindfulness; SE; CBT's decatastrophizing |
| **Cognitive restructuring** | Most explicit in CBT; implicitly present in others |
| **Exposure (broadly defined)** | Facing fear is a common factor in all therapies, expressed in different forms |
| **New relational experience** | Most central to psychoanalysis, humanistic, and attachment-focused approaches |
| **Narrative restructuring** | Narrative therapy; psychoanalysis; CBT (different emphases) |
| **Somatic work** | SE; Gestalt; EMDR; MBSR |
| **Skill acquisition** | DBT; CBT; ACT |
| **Meaning-making** | Existential; logotherapy; narrative therapy |

## 4. Principles for Selecting a Therapy

### Factors to prioritize (in order of importance)

1. **Client preference and cultural fit** — always first
2. **Degree of evidence** — specific diagnoses have specific high-quality evidence
3. **Therapist's genuine competency** — doing what you truly know is safer than doing what you've only learned once
4. **Client's level of psychological functioning** — lower functioning → prioritize support; higher functioning → exploratory approaches viable
5. **Time and resource constraints** — limited sessions → SFBT / brief CBT
6. **Quality of the alliance** — weak alliance → build the alliance first; defer deep interventions

### When to consider integration or switching therapies

- No change after 8–12 sessions: re-evaluate treatment fit
- Client explicitly states the current approach doesn't feel right: take it seriously; don't get defensive
- New clinical information emerges (trauma history, personality features) that changes the case formulation
- Therapist feels the method is exhausted: may need to integrate a new perspective

### Correct use of the Lambert model

```
Average sources of change:
  40%  Client factors
  30%  Therapeutic relationship
  15%  Expectancy / placebo
  15%  Specific technique

Important qualifications:
  OCD + ERP     → technique weight is far above 15%
  PTSD + EMDR   → technique weight is far above 15%
  BPD + DBT     → technique (skills training) is a core component

→ The model says "relationship matters."
  It does not say "technique doesn't matter."

→ For specific diagnoses: follow evidence-based guidelines;
  do not substitute "relationship first" for effective technique.
```
