## 1. Psychoanalysis and Psychodynamic Therapy

### Essential introductions

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *Introductory Lectures on Psycho-Analysis* | Sigmund Freud | The classical foundation; the starting point for understanding the unconscious, defense mechanisms, and dream interpretation |
| *The Ego and the Id* | Sigmund Freud | The original text of the structural model (id / ego / superego) |
| *Object Relations in Psychoanalytic Theory* | Greenberg & Mitchell | A clear mapping from drive theory to relational theory; ideal for beginners building an integrated overview |
| *Introduction to Contemporary Psychoanalysis* | Greenberg | A systematic comparative introduction to the major psychoanalytic schools |

### Advanced reading

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *On Countertransference* | Paula Heimann et al. | The foundational essay collection establishing countertransference as a clinical instrument |
| *The Language of Psycho-Analysis* | Laplanche & Pontalis | The authoritative reference for psychoanalytic concepts |
| *The Technique and Practice of Psychoanalysis* | Ralph Greenson | The classic technical textbook for clinical practice |
| *Relational Concepts in Psychoanalysis* | Stephen Mitchell | The core text of relational psychoanalysis |
| *Psychoanalysis: The Impossible Profession* | Janet Malcolm | A cultural examination of the psychoanalytic tradition *(essential reading; maintain critical perspective)* |

### Self psychology (Kohut)

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *The Analysis of the Self* | Heinz Kohut | The founding work of self psychology; theory of narcissism and selfobjects |
| *The Restoration of the Self* | Heinz Kohut | Deepens the clinical applications of self psychology |

## 2. Object Relations and Attachment Theory

### Core object relations

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *Love, Hate and Reparation* | Melanie Klein & Joan Riviere | Introduction to Kleinian theory; paranoid-schizoid and depressive positions |
| *Playing and Reality* | Donald Winnicott | Transitional objects, the "good enough" mother, true and false self; clinically rich |
| *The Family and Individual Development* | Donald Winnicott | Clinical implications of early mother-infant relating |
| *Object Relations in Psychoanalytic Theory* | Greenberg & Mitchell | Systematic comparison of two major traditions |
| *Borderline Conditions and Pathological Narcissism* | Otto Kernberg | Authoritative psychoanalytic text on treating BPD and NPD |

### Attachment theory

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *Attachment and Loss* (3 vols.) | John Bowlby | The original source; understanding the secure base and separation anxiety |
| *Loss: Sadness and Depression* | John Bowlby | Classic treatment of grief and attachment |
| *A Secure Base* | Jeremy Holmes | Translating attachment theory directly into clinical practice |
| *Attachment in Adulthood* | Mario Mikulincer & Philip Shaver | Authoritative review of adult attachment research |
| *Adult Attachment Interview* | Mary Main et al. | Original materials establishing the AAI paradigm |

## 3. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *Cognitive Therapy of Depression* | Aaron Beck | The founding work of CBT; the model for structured treatment protocols |
| *Cognitive Behavior Therapy: Basics and Beyond* | Judith Beck | The most systematic textbook; highly accessible |
| *Cognitive Behavior Therapy: Science and Practice* | Jesse Wright et al. | A comprehensive text integrating case examples |
| *Reason and Emotion in Psychotherapy* | Albert Ellis | The classic REBT text (ABC model) |

### Diagnosis-specific applications

| Title | Author | Core value |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| *Cognitive Therapy of Anxiety Disorders* | Clark & Beck | Authoritative CBT guide for the anxiety spectrum |
| *Cognitive-Behavioral Treatment of OCD* | Salkovskis et al. | ERP theory and practice for OCD |
| *Treatment for Chronic Depression* | McCullough | CBASP — an approach designed specifically for chronic depression |
| *Schema Therapy* | Jeffrey Young et al. | An advanced CBT model targeting personality disorders and early maladaptive schemas |

## Recommended Reading Pathways

### Novice therapist (0–2 years)

```
Core framework       →  Judith Beck's Cognitive Behavior Therapy
                        OR Carl Rogers' On Becoming a Person

Therapeutic relation →  Yalom's Existential Psychotherapy (case narratives)

Trauma basics        →  Judith Herman's Trauma and Recovery

Neuroscience         →  Daniel Siegel's Mindsight (accessible edition)
```

### Advancing therapist (2–5 years)

```
Dynamic theory       →  Greenberg & Mitchell's Object Relations in Psychoanalytic Theory

Deepening attachment →  Jeremy Holmes's A Secure Base

Somatic integration  →  Bessel van der Kolk's The Body Keeps the Score

Supervision theory   →  Hawkins & Shohet's Supervision in the Helping Professions
```

### Seasoned therapist (5+ years)

```
Integrative view     →  Norcross's Handbook of Psychotherapy Integration

Existential depth    →  Yalom's Love's Executioner (case studies)

Deeper neuroscience  →  Allan Schore's Affect Regulation and the Origin of the Self

Personal orientation →  Follow your own tradition's foundational texts
```

---

> **Usage note:** When a therapist expresses confusion about a theoretical area, or wishes to deepen their knowledge of a specific modality, recommend 1–2 titles from the relevant section. Prioritize texts with high clinical applicability. Avoid recommending highly technical original texts to beginners.
